Affiliate Disclosure

Last updated: April 6, 2026

We believe in full transparency. This page explains exactly how LawnPro makes money from product recommendations and how that relationship works.

FTC Disclosure

In accordance with the Federal Trade Commission's guidelines (16 CFR Part 255), LawnPro discloses that we participate in affiliate advertising programs and may earn commissions from qualifying purchases made through links in our app and website.

This means: if you click a product link in LawnPro and make a purchase, we may receive a small commission at no additional cost to you.

Amazon Associates Program

LawnPro is a participant in the Amazon Services LLC Associates Program, an affiliate advertising program designed to provide a means for sites to earn advertising fees by advertising and linking to Amazon.com.

When you click an Amazon product link in LawnPro and make a purchase within 24 hours, we earn a commission of approximately 3–10% depending on the product category.

Sponsored Placements

Some products and brands in LawnPro are "Sponsored." This means the brand has paid for featured placement — for example, appearing as the featured partner card in your analysis results. Sponsored placements are always clearly labeled with a "Sponsored" badge.

Our Commitment to You

Sponsored placements and affiliate relationships never influence our AI-generated lawn analysis. Our AI assesses your lawn solely based on your photo and the details you provide — it has no knowledge of which products we earn money from.

Product recommendations are matched to your specific lawn issues. For example, if your analysis detects nitrogen deficiency, we show nitrogen fertilizer products. We would show those products whether or not we had an affiliate relationship with the brand.

We only recommend products that we believe are genuinely suitable for the lawn problem identified. If you have concerns about any specific recommendation, please reach out.
